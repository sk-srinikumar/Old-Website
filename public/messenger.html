Background

I’ve designed for a pretty popular messaging app, but I’ve never gotten to design an entire messenger UI myself. I scratched my itch by designing a substantial amount.



Design

Usually, messengers have a header bar at the top with a contact name and option buttons. Why not just have that stuff at the bottom of the screen where your eyes and thumbs hang out? We already know who we’re talking to anyway so the name and picture don’t need all that spotlight.

The arrow at the bottom shows that you can swipe up to expand your conversations list (which would be the home-screen).

Your brain is looking for social cues when talking to people. Like movements, positions, and weight. You lose that in digital conversations, so I thought I can add bits of that back with shadows. Ideally, almost every function and object would have subtle animations to add some non-distracting life. Little things add up, so it matters to me.

The addition of stickers and apps to messengers has made them more cluttered. Yet I’ve put them all under one button. You swipe one way to scroll through your most-used sticker and the other way to scroll through your most recent images. You can tap the arrows next to the labels to open a full-view gallery with categories.

I also had a few innovative ideas for a UI to communicate with commerce bots. But that’s a project for another day!

Fun fact: the proper phrasing is a UI not an UI!
